## Before You Begin

This week's lab is a standalone Bootstrap exercise — you will be working in a plain HTML file, **not** inside your Django project. You will need a code editor (e.g. VS Code) and a web browser.

The goal is to design and build a one-page portfolio website using the Bootstrap grid system and components covered in today's lecture.

---

## Exercise 1 — Design your One-Page Portfolio Website (10 min)

Using pen and paper or digital tools such as Sketch, Adobe Photoshop, PowerPoint or Proto.io, create a mock-up of a one-page website.
Use the following resource for inspiration and reference: 
https://onepagelove.com/inspiration/portfolio.

Your web page should contain the following:

- A Bootstrap navigation bar (with no links – or perhaps links to LinkedIn or similar social networks or other websites you are on) or a Bootstrap header;
- A lead paragraph (check out the Typography section in the Content section of the documentation), displayed at the top of the page, to show the key content on your website, such as a summary of the remaining information below;
- A biography section, using Bootstrap elements (such as Headers and Paragraphs), describing who you are and your skills;
- An experience section, using Bootstrap elements, showcasing your past projects. Don’t worry if you don’t have any projects in your portfolio right now, instead list projects you want to work on.
- A contact form section, using Bootstrap elements, describing ways to contact yourself such as phone, email or social media, but also with a contact ‘form’.

Obviously, your design (on ‘paper’) won’t be using Bootstrap but consider the components available to you while you do so.
You can draw and label boxes as to what each of these things will be.
